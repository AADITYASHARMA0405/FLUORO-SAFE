WHO

World Health Organization

International Standard
1.5 mg/L

WHO Guidelines for Drinking-water Quality recommend fluoride should not exceed 1.5 mg/L. Concentrations above this cause significant risk of dental and skeletal fluorosis.

BIS

Bureau of Indian Standards

IS 10500:2012
1.0 mg/L

BIS acceptable limit for fluoride is 1.0 mg/L with a maximum permissible limit of 1.5 mg/L in the absence of an alternate drinking water source.

Health Effects of Excess Fluoride

Understanding the dangers of fluoride contamination in water

🦷

Dental Fluorosis

> 1.5 mg/L

Causes white spots, pitting, and brown staining on tooth enamel. Affects children during tooth development significantly.

🦴

Skeletal Fluorosis

> 4 mg/L

Chronic exposure leads to joint pain, stiffness, bone deformities, and calcification of ligaments. Can be crippling and irreversible.

🧠

Neurological Effects

> 2 mg/L

Studies link excess fluoride to reduced IQ in children, cognitive impairment, memory problems, and neurodevelopmental issues.

🫘

Kidney Damage

> 3 mg/L

High fluoride accumulation damages kidney tissue, impairs renal function, and increases risk of kidney stones over long-term exposure.

🦋

Thyroid Disruption

> 2 mg/L

Fluoride can suppress thyroid function, leading to hypothyroidism. Affects metabolism, energy levels, and hormonal balance.

🧬

Reproductive Issues

> 3 mg/L

Research indicates high fluoride can affect fertility, hormone levels, and reproductive health in both males and females.

Safety Precautions

Measures to protect yourself from fluoride contamination

01

Regular Water Testing

Test your water supply regularly for fluoride concentration using certified labs or IoT sensors. Monitor seasonal variations in groundwater quality.

02

Use Defluoridation Filters

Install activated alumina or reverse osmosis (RO) filters specifically designed to remove fluoride. Ensure regular maintenance and filter replacement.

03

Avoid Groundwater in High-F Areas

In known fluoride-endemic regions, prefer treated municipal water supply over borewells and untested groundwater sources for drinking and cooking.

04

Calcium & Vitamin C Intake

Increase dietary calcium (milk, cheese) and Vitamin C (citrus fruits) which help mitigate fluoride absorption and promote excretion.

05

Rainwater Harvesting

Rainwater is naturally fluoride-free. Implement rainwater harvesting systems as an alternative safe water source for drinking purposes.

06

Community Awareness

Educate communities about fluoride risks, advocate for centralized defluoridation plants, and support government initiatives for safe water supply.
